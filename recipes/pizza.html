Margerita Pizza

.

Description

A Margherita pizza is a classic Italian pizza made with a thin, soft crust topped with simple ingredients: tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, fresh basil leaves, a drizzle of olive oil, and a pinch of salt. The colors of the toppings—red from the tomato, white from the mozzarella, and green from the basil—represent the Italian flag, making it both a delicious and patriotic dish. It's known for its fresh, vibrant flavors and is one of the most popular and traditional pizza styles.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 packet (2 ¼ teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • ¾ cup warm water
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • ½ cup tomato sauce (San Marzano tomatoes preferred)
  • 1 ½ cups fresh mozzarella (sliced)
  • Fresh basil leaves
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil (for drizzling)
  • ½ teaspoon salt (for seasoning)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dough: Mix warm water, yeast, and sugar. Let it sit for 5–10 minutes until it foams. Add flour, salt, and olive oil, then knead into a smooth dough. Cover and let it rise for 1 hour.
  2. Preheat Oven: Set your oven to 475°F (245°C) and place a pizza stone or baking sheet inside to heat up.
  3. Roll Out the Dough: Once risen, roll out the dough into a 10–12 inch circle on a floured surface.
  4. Assemble the Pizza: Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border. Arrange mozzarella slices on top.
  5. Bake: Transfer the pizza onto the hot baking sheet or stone. Bake for 10–12 minutes until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly.
  6. Add Final Touches: Remove from the oven, top with fresh basil leaves, and drizzle with olive oil.
  7. Serve & Enjoy! Let it cool for a minute, slice, and serve hot.
